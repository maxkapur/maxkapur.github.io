Euler’s method

Refererence: Paul’s Online Notes, section 2.9

Euler’s method is a conceptually simple way of estimating the value of a first-order differential equation. It is especially accurate for $t$-values close to the initial condition. It works by putting the DE in terms of $y'$ and then taking discrete steps of size $h$ in the direction of the tangent line at time $t+hn$.

I use Euler’s method to create graphs of two of Paul’s example functions.

Example 1: $\qquad y′ + 2y = 2− e^{−4t} \qquad y(0)=1$¶

The solution, unknown to us, is $y(t)=1+\frac{1}{2}e^{4t}\frac{1}{2}e^{2t}$.

    ex1out= np.append(ex1out,new,axis=0)
    ex1out[i,2]= ex1(ex1out[i,0],ex1out[i,1])

Let’s make a similar similar to Paul’s. The first column indicates the $t$-value, the second indicates the estimate for $y(t)$, and the third indicates the evaluation of the DE at the given $t$ and $y$—that is, the slope of our next step.

Example 3: $\qquad y' - y = - \frac{1}{2}{e}^{\frac{t}{2}}\sin(5t) + 5e^{\frac{t}{2}}\cos(5t)\qquad y( 0 ) = 0$¶

The solution, unknown to us, is $y(t)= e^{t/2}\sin(5t)$.
